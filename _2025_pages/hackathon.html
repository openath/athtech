Revolutionizing Athletics Through Technology

AthTech presents TrHackathon, a global virtual innovation challenge calling upon the brightest minds in technology and sports to transform how fans experience athletics. This competition brings together data scientists, developers, designers, and any technology/athletics enthusiasts to create groundbreaking solutions that will shape the future of track and field, combined events and road races.

We're seeking visionary concepts that leverage emerging technologies to enhance fan engagement, athlete performance understanding and competition visualizations. Whether through immersive XR experiences, AI-driven analytics, wearable integrations, or novel data visualizations, your ideas will help evolve athletics.

Built on Top of Real Competition Data

Participants will be provided with real competition datasets that will be available in a GitHub repository. Teams are required to build their ideas and solutions using the official datasets. Furthermore, they also can consider external libraries or APIs, or any other technologies.

We ❤️ Innovative Ideas

We welcome ideas of projects to cover any area of innovation in athletics competition, including:

  • Immersive fan experiences (e.g., VR, AR solutions)
  • Next-Generation Data Visualization (e.g., intuitive dashboards, broadcast graphics, or stadium displays)
  • Competition storytelling (e.g., narrative-driven tools that help fans understand athletics events, particularly in disciplines like combined events, jumps or throws)
  • Real-Time engagement solutions (e.g., predictive analytics, interactive voting, or personalized content delivery)

Don't see your idea here? Don't worry, we welcome all innovative concepts

What to Deliver

Participants are expected to generate ideas for solutions, products, and services to increase the efficiency of competition management, fan engagement, or any other aspect contributing to the sport's digitalization.

The projects will be (briefly) documented in a website, PDF, presentation, or code repository, available on the web. If the project is a proof-of-concept or pilot, we recommended to link the code repository, app or demonstrative videos from the document.

The public document must include at least the following sections: Abstract with the summary; Description of the project with use cases, challenges that solve, etc.; Innovation, with creative and unique aspects of the solution; Impact, including the potential of the project to transform athletics experiences (and who will benefit); Feasibility, with the description of if the idea is realistic or what is needed to create it.

Tips:

  • Be creative! Any outcome will be welcome, but have a look at the evaluation criteria first.
  • The simpler, the better. One or two paragraphs would be enough.
  • Add pictures or videos to help us understand how cool your idea is.
  • Ask in the forum.

The Process

  1. Register your interest by starting a thread in the forum and tell us about your ideas. This does not commit you to going ahead.
  2. Be ready for an interim check-in around 14 July. We can either meet you online 1:1, or you can give a short 5-minute update during a public online meeting, as you prefer.
  3. Submit your project by 1st September.
  4. Be ready for an on-screen presentation to the judges in the first two weeks of September.

Teams of 1~3 members are welcome.

The Prize

The winning team will receive tickets to the next European Championships (Birmingham, UK, 2026); and will be invited to present in person at AthTech, with all expenses (from within Europe) covered by European Athletics.

Data & Tools for the Challenge

The projects must use data available in the official TrHackathon repository. Participants may also use their own and public external sources or tools (e.g., WikiData and OpenStreetMap), complying with the applicable licenses and use terms.

These projects must explicitly mention the data sources, tools, and libraries used.
